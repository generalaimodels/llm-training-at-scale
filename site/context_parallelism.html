Tensor Parallelism (TP) and Sequence Parallelism (SP)

1. Motivation: Why Tensor Parallelism?

ZeRO (Zero Redundancy Optimizer) successfully shards parameters, gradients, and optimizer states across GPUs. However, activation memory — the intermediate tensors produced during the forward pass — remains replicated on every device. As model size and sequence length grow, activation memory dominates the per-GPU memory budget, creating an insurmountable bottleneck.

Tensor Parallelism (TP) resolves this by sharding not only parameters, gradients, and optimizer states but also activations — and critically, it does so without requiring a full gather of all shards before computation. Instead, TP exploits the algebraic structure of matrix multiplication to distribute computation natively across devices.

2. Mathematical Foundations of Tensor Parallelism

Tensor parallelism rests on two fundamental decomposition properties of matrix multiplication. Given matrices A∈Rm×kA \in \mathbb{R}^{m \times k}A∈Rm×kandB∈Rk×nB \in \mathbb{R}^{k \times n}B∈Rk×n:

2.1 Column-wise Decomposition (Splitting BBB by Columns)

Partition BBBintoNNN column blocks:

B=[B1B2⋯BN],Bi∈Rk×(n/N) B = \begin{bmatrix} B_1 & B_2 & \cdots & B_N \end{bmatrix}, \quad B_i \in \mathbb{R}^{k \times (n/N)} B=[B1​​B2​​⋯​BN​​],Bi​∈Rk×(n/N)

Then:

A⋅B=A⋅[B1B2⋯BN]=[AB1AB2⋯ABN] A \cdot B = A \cdot \begin{bmatrix} B_1 & B_2 & \cdots & B_N \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} AB_1 & AB_2 & \cdots & AB_N \end{bmatrix} A⋅B=A⋅[B1​​B2​​⋯​BN​​]=[AB1​​AB2​​⋯​ABN​​]

Each partial product ABi∈Rm×(n/N)AB_i \in \mathbb{R}^{m \times (n/N)}ABi​∈Rm×(n/N)can be computed independently on GPUiii, and the final result is obtained by concatenating (all-gather) the partial outputs along the column dimension.

Partition AAAintoNNNcolumn blocks andBBBintoNNN row blocks:

A=[A1A2⋯AN],Ai∈Rm×(k/N) A = \begin{bmatrix} A_1 & A_2 & \cdots & A_N \end{bmatrix}, \quad A_i \in \mathbb{R}^{m \times (k/N)} A=[A1​​A2​​⋯​AN​​],Ai​∈Rm×(k/N)

Then:

A⋅B=∑i=1NAiBi A \cdot B = \sum_{i=1}^{N} A_i B_i A⋅B=i=1∑N​Ai​Bi​

Each partial product AiBi∈Rm×nA_i B_i \in \mathbb{R}^{m \times n}Ai​Bi​∈Rm×nis computed independently on GPUiii, and the final result is obtained by summing (all-reduce) the partial outputs.

In a neural network linear layer, the operation is expressed as:

where:

3.1 Procedure

W=[W1W2⋯WN],Wi∈Rh×(h′/N) W = \begin{bmatrix} W_1 & W_2 & \cdots & W_N \end{bmatrix}, \quad W_i \in \mathbb{R}^{h \times (h'/N)} W=[W1​​W2​​⋯​WN​​],Wi​∈Rh×(h′/N)

5. Tensor Parallelism in a Transformer Block

A standard Transformer decoder layer comprises two sub-blocks:

  1. Multi-Head Attention (MHA) block
  2. Feedforward MLP block

Each is amenable to tensor parallelism due to the existence of naturally independent dimensions.

5.1 Feedforward MLP Block

A typical MLP in a Transformer consists of two linear projections with a nonlinearity:

MLP(X)=GELU(XW1)⋅W2 \text{MLP}(X) = \text{GELU}(X W_1) \cdot W_2 MLP(X)=GELU(XW1​)⋅W2​

where W1∈Rh×4hW_1 \in \mathbb{R}^{h \times 4h}W1​∈Rh×4h(up-projection) andW2∈R4h×hW_2 \in \mathbb{R}^{4h \times h}W2​∈R4h×h (down-projection).

Optimal TP strategy for MLP:

Layer Parallelism Type Rationale
W1W_1W1​ (FC1) Column-linear Split output dimension; GELU applied independently per shard
W2W_2W2​ (FC2) Row-linear Accepts sharded input from FC1; produces full output via all-reduce

Why this ordering (column → row) is superior to (row → column):

  • Column-linear first requires only a broadcast (or no-op if inputs are already synchronized) to distribute XXX.
  • Row-linear second requires an all-reduce to combine results.
  • Total: 1 broadcast + 1 all-reduce per MLP block in forward pass.
  • The reverse ordering (row → column) would require an intermediate all-reduce between the two linear layers plus additional communication, making it strictly less efficient.

The forward computation on GPU iii proceeds as:

Zi=GELU(X⋅W1(i)),W1(i)∈Rh×(4h/N) Z_i = \text{GELU}\left(X \cdot W_1^{(i)}\right), \quad W_1^{(i)} \in \mathbb{R}^{h \times (4h/N)} Zi​=GELU(X⋅W1(i)​),W1(i)​∈Rh×(4h/N)
Yi=Zi⋅W2(i),W2(i)∈R(4h/N)×h Y_i = Z_i \cdot W_2^{(i)}, \quad W_2^{(i)} \in \mathbb{R}^{(4h/N) \times h} Yi​=Zi​⋅W2(i)​,W2(i)​∈R(4h/N)×h
Y=∑i=1NYi(all-reduce) Y = \sum_{i=1}^{N} Y_i \quad \text{(all-reduce)} Y=i=1∑N​Yi​(all-reduce)

The GELU nonlinearity is applied element-wise within each shard, requiring no cross-GPU communication — this is precisely why column-linear sharding of W1W_1W1​ is essential (sharding along the output dimension preserves the independence of the nonlinearity).

5.2 Multi-Head Attention (MHA) Block

The attention mechanism computes:

Attention(Q,K,V)=softmax(QK⊤dk)V \text{Attention}(Q, K, V) = \text{softmax}\left(\frac{QK^\top}{\sqrt{d_k}}\right) V Attention(Q,K,V)=softmax(dk​​QK⊤​)V

where Q=XWQQ = X W_QQ=XWQ​, K=XWKK = X W_KK=XWK​, V=XWVV = X W_VV=XWV​, and dk=h/nheadsd_k = h / n_{\text{heads}}dk​=h/nheads​ is the per-head dimension.

Natural parallelism: Each attention head operates independently. With nheadsn_{\text{heads}}nheads​attention heads distributed acrossNNNGPUs, each GPU handlesnheads/Nn_{\text{heads}} / Nnheads​/N heads.

Projection Parallelism Type Rationale
WQ,WK,WVW_Q, W_K, W_VWQ​,WK​,WV​ Column-linear Each column shard corresponds to a subset of attention heads
WOW_OWO​ (output projection) Row-linear Accepts concatenated head outputs (already sharded); produces full hidden via all-reduce

The communication pattern is identical to the MLP block:

  • Forward: 1 broadcast (or no-op) + 1 all-reduce per attention block
  • Backward: Conjugate operations

In GQA, the number of key/value heads nkv_headsn_{\text{kv\_heads}}nkv_heads​is smaller than the number of query headsnattention_headsn_{\text{attention\_heads}}nattention_heads​:

nattention_heads≥nkv_heads n_{\text{attention\_heads}} \geq n_{\text{kv\_heads}} nattention_heads​≥nkv_heads​

6. Communication Analysis of Tensor Parallelism

6.1 Communication Primitives in the Critical Path

For each Transformer decoder layer (MHA + MLP), the forward pass requires:

Forward: 2×all-reduce(one for MHA, one for MLP) \text{Forward: } 2 \times \text{all-reduce} \quad \text{(one for MHA, one for MLP)} Forward: 2×all-reduce(one for MHA, one for MLP)
Backward: 2×all-reduce(conjugate operations) \text{Backward: } 2 \times \text{all-reduce} \quad \text{(conjugate operations)} Backward: 2×all-reduce(conjugate operations)

These all-reduce operations sit directly on the critical path of computation — they cannot be trivially overlapped with compute because subsequent operations (e.g., LayerNorm, residual addition) depend on the synchronized result.

Critical path: The longest chain of sequentially dependent operations determining the minimum wall-clock time for a forward or backward pass.

6.2 Communication Volume

For an all-reduce of a tensor of size MMMacrossNNN GPUs, the total communication volume (using ring all-reduce) is:

Vall-reduce=2⋅N−1N⋅M V_{\text{all-reduce}} = 2 \cdot \frac{N-1}{N} \cdot M Vall-reduce​=2⋅NN−1​⋅M

For the MLP block, M=b⋅s⋅hM = b \cdot s \cdot hM=b⋅s⋅h, giving:

VMLP=2⋅N−1N⋅b⋅s⋅hper layer (forward) V_{\text{MLP}} = 2 \cdot \frac{N-1}{N} \cdot b \cdot s \cdot h \quad \text{per layer (forward)} VMLP​=2⋅NN−1​⋅b⋅s⋅hper layer (forward)

7. Memory Benefits of Tensor Parallelism

With TP degree NNN, the per-GPU memory for a linear layer with weight W∈Rh×h′W \in \mathbb{R}^{h \times h'}W∈Rh×h′ is:

7.1 Parameters

Params per GPU=h×h′N \text{Params per GPU} = \frac{h \times h'}{N} Params per GPU=Nh×h′​

7.2 Gradients

Gradients per GPU=h×h′N \text{Gradients per GPU} = \frac{h \times h'}{N} Gradients per GPU=Nh×h′​

7.3 Optimizer States (Adam)

Adam maintains first moment mmmand second momentvvv, each the same size as the parameters:

Optimizer states per GPU=2×h×h′N(in fp32, so 2×4×h×h′N bytes) \text{Optimizer states per GPU} = \frac{2 \times h \times h'}{N} \quad \text{(in fp32, so } \frac{2 \times 4 \times h \times h'}{N} \text{ bytes)} Optimizer states per GPU=N2×h×h′​(in fp32, so N2×4×h×h′​ bytes)

7.4 Activations (Partial Benefit)

Intermediate activations within TP regions are sharded:

Activation per GPU (TP region)=b⋅s⋅h′N(for column-linear output) \text{Activation per GPU (TP region)} = \frac{b \cdot s \cdot h'}{N} \quad \text{(for column-linear output)} Activation per GPU (TP region)=Nb⋅s⋅h′​(for column-linear output)

However, operations like LayerNorm and dropout still require the full activation tensor b×s×hb \times s \times hb×s×h, limiting the activation memory savings. This is the precise limitation that Sequence Parallelism addresses.

8. Sequence Parallelism (SP)

8.1 Core Idea

Sequence parallelism shards the activations along the sequence dimension sss for operations that are outside the tensor-parallel regions — specifically LayerNorm and dropout.

These operations require the full hidden dimension hhh(e.g., LayerNorm computes statistics acrosshhh), so they cannot be sharded along hhh. However, they operate independently across sequence positions, making sharding along sss natural.

8.2 LayerNorm Definition

LayerNorm(x)=γ⋅x−μσ2+ϵ+β \text{LayerNorm}(x) = \gamma \cdot \frac{x - \mu}{\sqrt{\sigma^2 + \epsilon}} + \beta LayerNorm(x)=γ⋅σ2+ϵ​x−μ​+β

where:

μ=1h∑j=1hxj,σ2=1h∑j=1h(xj−μ)2 \mu = \frac{1}{h} \sum_{j=1}^{h} x_j, \quad \sigma^2 = \frac{1}{h} \sum_{j=1}^{h} (x_j - \mu)^2 μ=h1​j=1∑h​xj​,σ2=h1​j=1∑h​(xj​−μ)2

Both μ\muμandσ2\sigma^2σ2are computed across the hidden dimensionhhh for each sequence position independently. Therefore:

  • Cannot shard along hhh (would produce incorrect statistics)
  • Can shard along sss (each position is independent)

8.3 Transition Operations: Conjugate Pairs f/f∗f/f^*f/f∗andg/g∗g/g^*g/g∗

The interplay between TP regions and SP regions requires carefully designed communication operators:

TP Region Operators (fffandf∗f^*f∗)

Pass fff f∗f^*f∗
Forward No-op (activations already replicated) All-reduce (synchronize partial results)
Backward All-reduce (synchronize gradients) No-op (gradients already replicated)

fffandf∗f^*f∗ are conjugate pairs: when one is a no-op, the other is an all-reduce, and vice versa across forward and backward passes.

SP ↔ TP Transition Operators (gggandg∗g^*g∗)

Pass ggg g∗g^*g∗
Forward All-gather (reconstruct full sequence for TP) Reduce-scatter (shard sequence for SP)
Backward Reduce-scatter (distribute gradients) All-gather (reconstruct gradients)

gggandg∗g^*g∗ are also conjugate pairs.

8.4 Data Flow Through a Transformer Layer with TP+SP

Consider a two-GPU setup (N=2N = 2N=2) with input X∈Rb×s×hX \in \mathbb{R}^{b \times s \times h}X∈Rb×s×h:

Step 1: LayerNorm (SP region)

Each GPU holds Xi∗∈Rb×(s/N)×hX_i^* \in \mathbb{R}^{b \times (s/N) \times h}Xi∗​∈Rb×(s/N)×h (sharded along sequence).

Each GPU computes LayerNorm independently on its chunk:

Yi∗=LayerNorm(Xi∗)∈Rb×(s/N)×h Y_i^* = \text{LayerNorm}(X_i^*) \in \mathbb{R}^{b \times (s/N) \times h} Yi∗​=LayerNorm(Xi∗​)∈Rb×(s/N)×h

Step 2: SP → TP transition (ggg: all-gather)

Reconstruct the full sequence on each GPU:

Y=AllGather(Y1∗,Y2∗,…,YN∗)∈Rb×s×h Y = \text{AllGather}(Y_1^*, Y_2^*, \ldots, Y_N^*) \in \mathbb{R}^{b \times s \times h} Y=AllGather(Y1∗​,Y2∗​,…,YN∗​)∈Rb×s×h

Step 3: Column-linear / FC1 (TP region)

Each GPU computes with its column shard W1(i)W_1^{(i)}W1(i)​:

Zi∗=GELU(Y⋅W1(i))∈Rb×s×(4h/N) Z_i^* = \text{GELU}\left(Y \cdot W_1^{(i)}\right) \in \mathbb{R}^{b \times s \times (4h/N)} Zi∗​=GELU(Y⋅W1(i)​)∈Rb×s×(4h/N)

Step 4: Row-linear / FC2 (TP region)

Each GPU computes with its row shard W2(i)W_2^{(i)}W2(i)​:

Oi=Zi∗⋅W2(i)∈Rb×s×h O_i = Z_i^* \cdot W_2^{(i)} \in \mathbb{R}^{b \times s \times h} Oi​=Zi∗​⋅W2(i)​∈Rb×s×h

Step 5: TP → SP transition (g∗g^*g∗: reduce-scatter)

Oj∗=ReduceScatter(O1,O2,…,ON)∈Rb×(s/N)×h O_j^* = \text{ReduceScatter}(O_1, O_2, \ldots, O_N) \in \mathbb{R}^{b \times (s/N) \times h} Oj∗​=ReduceScatter(O1​,O2​,…,ON​)∈Rb×(s/N)×h

This simultaneously:

  1. Reduces (sums) the partial row-linear outputs for correctness
  2. Scatters the result along the sequence dimension for the subsequent SP region

9. Communication Equivalence: TP vs. TP+SP

9.1 Per-Layer Communication Count

Method Forward Operations per Layer
TP only 2 × all-reduce
TP + SP 2 × all-gather + 2 × reduce-scatter

9.2 Why They Are Equivalent

A single all-reduce can be decomposed into:

all-reduce=reduce-scatter+all-gather \text{all-reduce} = \text{reduce-scatter} + \text{all-gather} all-reduce=reduce-scatter+all-gather

Therefore, 2 all-reduce operations have the same communication volume as 2 all-gather + 2 reduce-scatter operations:

2×Vall-reduce=2×(Vreduce-scatter+Vall-gather)=2×Vall-gather+2×Vreduce-scatter 2 \times V_{\text{all-reduce}} = 2 \times (V_{\text{reduce-scatter}} + V_{\text{all-gather}}) = 2 \times V_{\text{all-gather}} + 2 \times V_{\text{reduce-scatter}} 2×Vall-reduce​=2×(Vreduce-scatter​+Vall-gather​)=2×Vall-gather​+2×Vreduce-scatter​

TP+SP introduces zero additional communication overhead relative to vanilla TP, while providing strictly superior activation memory savings.

9.3 Backward Pass

The backward pass uses the conjugate of each forward operation:

no-op⟷all-reduce,all-gather⟷reduce-scatter \text{no-op} \longleftrightarrow \text{all-reduce}, \quad \text{all-gather} \longleftrightarrow \text{reduce-scatter} no-op⟷all-reduce,all-gather⟷reduce-scatter

Thus, backward communication cost is also identical between TP and TP+SP.

10. Gradient Synchronization Notes

10.1 LayerNorm Weights

In TP+SP, after an all-gather, each TP rank processes the same activations through the TP region. This means:

  • LayerNorm weights see the same forward activations on every rank after all-gather.
  • Therefore, LayerNorm weight gradients are naturally identical across TP ranks.
  • No all-reduce is needed for LayerNorm weight gradients during the backward pass.

However, in the SP region, each rank processes a different sequence chunk:

  • LayerNorm gradients differ across ranks.
  • An all-reduce of LayerNorm gradients is required, but this is negligible since LayerNorm has only 2h2h2hparameters (γ\gammaγandβ\betaβ).

10.2 Dropout Synchronization

Dropout in SP regions operates on different sequence chunks per rank. To ensure deterministic behavior and reproducibility:

Seeddropout must be synchronized across all TP ranks \text{Seed}_{\text{dropout}} \text{ must be synchronized across all TP ranks} Seeddropout​ must be synchronized across all TP ranks

11. Remaining Limitations of TP + SP
